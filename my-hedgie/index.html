Chestnut the Hedgehog

This is my (former) hedgehog Chestnut. She unfortunately became really aggressive and needed to be returned to the breeder. Despite that, she is still extremely cute and would love to share some information about her for anybody interested in hedgehogs.

About Chestnut

Chestnut is an African Pygmy Hedgehog. I got her when she was about one and a half months old. She was about the size of a hamster when I got her (without the quills). Since her quills were almost always out she was probably around the size of a large apple. Hedgehogs are nocturnal animals so she normally would not wake up until I went to bed since the lights meant it was daytime to her. I had to return Chestnut to the breeder because she started biting my wife and I in order to get her way, which is abnormal for somewhat domesticated hedgehogs.

Things to know about hedgehogs

  • They are relatively blind and rely heavily on their sense of smell.
  • They traditionally eat bugs in the wild but will eat a variety of fruits, vegetables, and meats.
  • Hedgehogs are very active creatures and spend a lot of time moving.
  • Hedgehogs are smart enough to learn how to use a litter box.

Words of Warning...

For anyone interested in owning a hedgehog after this here are some things to watch out for:

  • Hedgehogs need around an hour of your attention everyday or they will "desocialize".
  • Like other small animals, they poo A TON.
  • In nature they go to the bathroom while running which domestic hedgehogs like to emulate while on their wheel creating a poo tornado (It's as disgusting as it sounds).
  • The first few months are extremely hard since they do not know you and you will get stuck with quills everyday.
